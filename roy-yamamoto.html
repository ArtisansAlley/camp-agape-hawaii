Roy, I know you’re going to make a difference in the world, so I’m going to cut you free today and give you back your freedom.

No one would know the impact of how one man’s life of crime would change a whole community with one child at a time….

This is a story of how God uses an ordinary criminal to do the extraordinary work of sharing the gospel. But it’s more than that even, it’s changing the lives of future generations of the children of incarcerated parents by taking them off the path of destruction and leading them on the path of righteousness.

It was 1993, and Roy had a visit from his daughter, Kristen, who was accompanied by his sister but she had a stuffed Turtle in her hand. One that was given to her through Angel Tree on behalf of her incarcerated father. To this day, she still has that turtle. But it gave Roy a profound moment to ponder for years and the reason he would start the ministry which is now known as Camp Agape.

In 1997, after two court appeals and the prosecution securing an 80 year double life sentence for Roy, the Judge looked up at Roy in court and said, “Roy, I know you’re going to make a difference in the world, so I’m going to cut you free today and give you back your freedom.”

Shortly after his release, Roy found a church to attend, got plugged in and within a year became its Prison Ministry Chaplain for a mega church on Oahu. Throughout all of this Roy never forgot that profound moment years earlier. And so Roy decided to start a program for inmates who were just released from prison. Then he started Angel Tree Christmas events where he would invite children and families of incarcerated. And then he have big carnival events for the families where over 5000 would attend. But God kept telling him, I want you to do more. Roy took two of his brothers and attended a conference on the mainland where he was introduced by a program of Camps that churches would do for the children of their incarcerated. And that’s when the profound moment was defined. When Roy returned, he decided its time to do a Camp. And not just any camp. This would be a special 4-day Camp for children of incarcerated parents where they would learn how to become positive role models for their families and their communities, but more importantly, they 2 would learn about “Agape” God’s unconditional love. And thus, Camp Agape was born.

When he started the camp, he saw kids who would come so hungry that they would hoard food until they realized that everything at the camp was for them because someone, this big God who is everywhere loves them.
