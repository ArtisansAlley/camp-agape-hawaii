Testimony of

Pastor Roy Yamamoto

“Roy, I know you’re going to make a difference in the world, so I’m going to cut you free today and give you back your freedom.”

No one could know the impact of one man, an ex-convict, and how he would change an entire community, one child at a time. Within a span of [number] years, Roy Yamamoto’s prison ministry changed the lives of future generations of the children of incarcerated parents by taking them off the path of self-destruction and leading them onto the path of righteousness.

In 1997, after two court appeals, prosecutors secured an 80 year double life sentence for Roy Yamamoto. During the trial, the judge looked up at him and told him, “Roy, I know you’re going to make a difference in the world, so I’m going to cut you free today and give you back your freedom.”

"...For Roy, this was a profound moment that seeded in him the idea to start a ministry for children of the incarcerated."

Shortly after his release, Roy started attending the [name] church, and within a year became its Prison Ministry Chaplain for a mega church on Oahu.

Roy’s story began in 1993 when his [age] daughter Kristen and his sister [name], visited him in prison. He noticed she held a stuffed turtle in her arms, given to her through a non-profit organization called Angel Tree, run by volunteers to deliver a gifts, the Gospel, and a personal message of love to children on behalf of their mom or dad in prison. The turtle was a reminder to her that her father was still watching over her, and she still has this turtle to this day. For Roy, this was a profound moment that seeded in him the idea to start a ministry for children of the incarcerated.

"...But in Roy’s own words, 'God kept telling him to do more'"

Roy began a program for inmates who were just released from prison, then worked with Angel Tree to organize Christmas events, inviting those same children and their families. He then organized large carnivals for the same families with attendance over 5000. But in Roy’s own words, “God kept telling him to do more.”

With two of his brothers, Roy attended the [name] conference on the mainland where he was introduced to a program run by their churches involving campouts where children of the incarcerated would attend. When Roy returned home, he decided to create a campout as well. But not just campout—a special four-day program for similar children where they would learn how to become positive role models at home and within their communities. But more importantly, they would learn about agape, God’s unconditional love. And thus, Camp Agape was born, on the grounds of [specify location.]

When Roy began the program, children would come so hungry that they would hoard food until they realized that everything at the camp was for them because of a God they barely know anything about, is everywhere and He loves them.
